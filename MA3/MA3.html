Up & Coming

Graduating High School

I graduated from Fort Dorchester High School in Charleston, SC in 2017 with the idea that I would go on to study engineering at USF and design rollercoasters for popular amusement parks like Disney. The truth was that I always wanted to study theatre and go on to be an actor. I have a huge passion for performance art and I was heavily involved in it during high school, singing in chorus and being in musicals. I'm too nervous to spend money on thousands of dollars on a theatre degree, though, so I decided to major in engineering and minor in theatre.

HCC, USF, and Changing Majors

I was supposed to start at USF during the summer of 2017, but I couldn't afford to start at that time and they told me that I could take some credits at a community college and return to USF the next the year. So begins my college journey at HCC in Tampa, living with my older sister. It was uneventful, taking general courses and a little lackluster. The most fun I had was in an acting class that I took on the downtown campus in spring. But in summer of 2018, I started at USF and it was average. It wasn't too bad. It wasn't until fall, that things started falling apart.

I was going through huge financial issues. The Financial Aid Office was absolutely horrible and unhelpful. They, somehow, never received my FAFSA and so labelled me as a student, forcing me to take out a private loan. There was a lot of lies, miscommunication, and outright hostile attitudes from the office. I eventually got it sorted it out, but it was too late as the toll of the entire situation dragged out through the semester and caused my grades to suffer, especially in Physics which I outright failed. I decided to leave USF due to mistreatment from the staff and return to HCC. I tried to retake Physics which I only barely passed with a D and that was when I realized that maybe engineering wasn't for me.

UCF and Web Design

I finished my AA in Arts at HCC and then my sister decided to move to Orlando and gave me the choice of staying to go to USF or apply to new college in Orlando. I considered applying to Full Sail for animation as cartoons and anime are big staples in my life, but I never animated anything and didn't have the portfolio or the money to go to such a school. So, I applied to UCF for Digital Media, Web Design and Social Platforms. I kind of liked it at first. It was okay, if a little boring. I liked the creative aspect of making a webpage look nice and having that creative liberty, but it was nothing like being on stage or holding a paint brush or sketch pencil. This semester, however, made me realize how much I genuinely hate coding. Maybe it's the pandemic, affecting how the classes were taught, but I dreaded attending Zoom calls and doing assignments. And, towards the end of the semester, I stopped showing up and skipped a few assignments.

My Future at UCF

I realized that coding and web design isn't something that I want to do for the rest of my life. I feel bored, tired, and just plain frustrated doing this and asking for help and receiving very little... Working in entertainment is what I've always wanted to do whether it be working behind the scenes or being in front of the audience, so I changed my major again to Radio and Television Communication with a minor in Theatre-Acting. I'm honestly very excited for a future in mass communication and getting back into acting, especially voice acting because it would allow me to work a little closer to animation. It took a while for me to realize what I wanted to do and there's been a lot of anxiety along the way, but I'm happy with my decisions so far.
